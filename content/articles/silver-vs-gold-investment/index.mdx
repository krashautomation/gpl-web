## Silver vs Gold: Investment Comparison

Both silver and gold are precious metals that have served as stores of value for centuries. However, they have distinct characteristics that make them suitable for different investment strategies.

![Side-by-side comparison of gold and silver bullion bars and coins](./metals-comparison.jpg)

### Key Differences

| Factor | Gold | Silver |
|--------|------|--------|
| Price per ounce | ~$2,800 | ~$32 |
| Volatility | Lower | Higher |
| Industrial use | 10% | 50% |
| Storage cost | Higher | Lower |
| Liquidity | Excellent | Good |

### When to Choose Gold

Gold is ideal for:

- **Wealth preservation**: Stable store of value over centuries
- **Crisis hedge**: Performs well during economic uncertainty
- **Portfolio insurance**: Low correlation with stocks and bonds
- **Large investments**: More compact storage for significant capital

### When to Choose Silver

Silver is ideal for:

- **Higher growth potential**: More volatile but greater upside
- **Industrial demand**: Growing use in solar panels and electronics
- **Affordability**: Lower entry point for small investors
- **Dual demand**: Both investment and industrial applications

### The Gold-Silver Ratio

The gold-silver ratio (how many ounces of silver equal one ounce of gold) historically averages around 50:1. When the ratio exceeds 70:1, silver may be undervalued relative to gold.

## Conclusion

Both metals have their place in a diversified portfolio. Many investors choose to hold both, with gold as the core position and silver for growth potential.
